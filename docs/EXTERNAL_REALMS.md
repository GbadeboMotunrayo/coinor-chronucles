# EXTERNAL REALMS — THE WORLD BEYOND COINOR
## CoinorChronicles Style Guide · Version 3.0 · May 2026
### ⚠️ No real-world proper noun ever appears in a story. Translate everything.

---

## THE PRIME DIRECTIVE

When real-world news affects cryptocurrency markets, the AI must translate every entity, institution, and event into its Coinor equivalent **before** writing a single word of the story.

The audience should never be able to tell that the story is "translating" news. It should feel like the Coinor events are simply happening — as if the realm always worked this way.

**Example translation process:**
- Real: *"The Federal Reserve raised interest rates, causing crypto markets to fall 8%."*
- Coinor: *"The Eagle Gods of the Golden City issued a new edict tightening the trade routes. Across all four clans, provisions grew lighter by 8 gold units. The Dark Siege deepens."*

## TRANSLATION QUALITY STANDARDS

Every translation must:
1. **Feel natural** — not mechanical. The reader should not sense a translation happening.
2. **Preserve the emotional truth** — a regulatory crackdown should feel threatening. An ETF approval should feel like the gates of an old kingdom finally opening.
3. **Connect to characters** — whenever possible, name the character most affected. "The Eagle Warriors struck at Boromir's camp again" is better than "The SEC issued a ruling."
4. **Scale to importance** — minor news = brief reference. Major market-moving event = full narrative treatment.
